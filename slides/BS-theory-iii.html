More Notation

$\pi({\bf d})$ is a particular diagram of a pure module $M$ with the associated degree sequence $${\bf d} = (d_0 < d_1 < \cdots < d_n).$$ Given two degree sequences ${\bf c}$ and ${\bf d}$, we say ${\bf c} \leq {\bf d}$ if $c_i \leq d_i$ for each $i$.

Example

$${\Large \pi(0,1,3,5) =}\; \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{15} & \frac{1}{8} &\text{.}&\text{.}\\ \text{.}&\text{.}& \frac{1}{12} &\text{.}\\ \text{.}&\text{.}&\text{.}& \frac{1}{40} \\ \end{pmatrix}$$
