Everyday I'm Shuffling

Let ${\bf c} = (0,3,5)$ and ${\bf d} = (0,1,6)$ be degree sequences. Take first differences: $$\Delta {\bf c} = (3,2), \Delta {\bf d} = (1,5).$$

The order-preserving permutations (or shuffles) of are

\[ ({\color{red} 3},{\color{red} 2},1,5),\, ({\color{red} 3},1,{\color{red} 2},5), \, ({\color{red} 3},1,5,{\color{red} 2}), \, \, (1,{\color{red} 3},{\color{red} 2},5), \,(1,{\color{red} 3},5,{\color{red} 2}), (1,5,{\color{red} 3},{\color{red} 2}). \]


Treat each shuffle as the first difference of a degree sequence starting with $c_0 + d_0 = 0$: $$\mathsf\Sigma((3,2,1,5),c_0+d_0) = (0,3,5,6,11).$$
